# Downloaded Papers

1. **Fishing for Magikarp: Automatically Detecting Under-trained Tokens in Large Language Models** (papers/2405.05417_fishing_for_magikarp.pdf)
   - Authors: Sander Land, Max Bartolo
   - Year: 2024 (EMNLP 2024)
   - arXiv: 2405.05417
   - Why relevant: Foundational paper on automated detection of under-trained tokens; provides methodology and extensive results for 88 models

2. **Glitch Tokens in Large Language Models: Categorization Taxonomy and Effective Detection** (papers/2404.09894_glitch_tokens_taxonomy.pdf)
   - Authors: Yuxi Li, Yi Liu, Gelei Deng, et al.
   - Year: 2024 (FSE 2024)
   - arXiv: 2404.09894
   - Why relevant: Comprehensive taxonomy of both glitch token types and model failure behaviors

3. **Improbable Bigrams Expose Vulnerabilities of Incomplete Tokens in Byte-Level Tokenizers** (papers/2410.23684_improbable_bigrams.pdf)
   - Authors: Eugene Jang, Kimin Lee, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2410.23684
   - Why relevant: Shows token combinations (not just single tokens) can cause hallucinations; 33-77% failure rates

4. **GlitchProber: Advancing Effective Detection and Mitigation of Glitch Tokens in Large Language Models** (papers/2408.04905_glitchprober.pdf)
   - Authors: Zhibo Zhang, et al.
   - Year: 2024 (ASE 2024)
   - arXiv: 2408.04905
   - Why relevant: Detection + mitigation approach; provides ground-truth glitch token datasets for 5 models

5. **GlitchMiner: Mining Glitch Tokens via Gradient-based Discrete Optimization** (papers/2410.15052_glitchminer.pdf)
   - Authors: Zihui Wu, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2410.15052
   - Why relevant: Complementary gradient-based detection approach; 19% improvement in precision

6. **Problematic Tokens: Tokenizer Bias in Large Language Models** (papers/2406.11214_problematic_tokens.pdf)
   - Authors: Jin Yang, Zhiqiang Wang, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2406.11214
   - Why relevant: Multilingual tongue-twister effects, especially Chinese/Korean in GPT-4o

7. **Coercing LLMs to Do and Reveal (Almost) Anything** (papers/2402.14020_coercing_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Jonas Geiping, Alex Stein, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2402.14020
   - Why relevant: Security implications of glitch tokens; broad attack surface systematization

8. **Evaluating the Robustness of Neural Language Models to Input Perturbations** (papers/2108.12237_robustness_perturbations.pdf)
   - Authors: Milad Moradi, Matthias Samwald
   - Year: 2021 (EMNLP 2021)
   - arXiv: 2108.12237
   - Why relevant: Model fragility to character-level perturbations (typos, misspellings)

9. **The Reversal Curse: LLMs trained on "A is B" fail to learn "B is A"** (papers/2309.12288_reversal_curse.pdf)
   - Authors: Lukas Berglund, et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2309.12288
   - Why relevant: Generalization failure as a different type of "tongue twister"
